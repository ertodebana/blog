Every developer eventually starts a blog. This is mine.

I've been writing iOS apps for a while now, and I've noticed that the most useful things I learn rarely make it out of my notes app. A tricky SwiftUI layout bug I spent three hours on, a neat `Combine` pattern a colleague showed me, a build-time trick that saved our CI pipeline — they all live in scattered files nobody will ever read, including me, a month from now.

So this is an experiment: a place to write things down properly, out in the open, in case they help someone else (or future me).

## What I'll write about

A few themes I already know I want to explore:

- **Swift & SwiftUI** — real problems I hit at work, and how I solved them
- **Tooling** — the small scripts and shortcuts that make a difference over a year
- **Shipping** — the non-code parts of building software: reviews, releases, on-call

I'll try to keep posts short and specific. I'd rather publish a 200-word note that solves one real problem than a 2000-word essay nobody finishes.

## About this site

The site is built with [Hugo](https://gohugo.io/) and the [PaperMod](https://github.com/adityatelange/hugo-PaperMod) theme, deployed to GitHub Pages through a GitHub Actions workflow. The source is [on GitHub](https://github.com/ertodebana/blog) — feel free to poke around, or steal the setup for your own blog.
